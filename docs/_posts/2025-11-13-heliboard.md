**HeliBoard — A Truly Private, Customizable Open-Source Keyboard for Android**

If you care about privacy, customization, and open-source software, you’ve probably spent time searching for a good Android keyboard that *isn’t constantly phoning home*.
Meet **HeliBoard** — a privacy-conscious and fully offline keyboard inspired by **AOSP** and **OpenBoard**, designed to put *you* back in control of your typing experience.

HeliBoard combines simplicity, transparency, and deep customization, all while keeping your data safe on your device.

## What Makes HeliBoard Special?

Unlike many popular Android keyboards, **HeliBoard doesn’t use Internet permission at all**. That means it literally *cannot* send data out — your words, typing habits, and clipboard history never leave your phone.

But this keyboard isn’t just about privacy — it’s also packed with thoughtful features for power users.

### Smarter Typing, On Your Terms

* Add your own **dictionaries** for suggestions and spell-check — or use community-maintained ones for your language.
* Even better, you can add **emoji or scientific symbol dictionaries** for smart suggestions beyond words.
* For Korean users, HeliBoard provides a special compatible dictionary to make suggestions work properly.

### Full Customization

* Choose from built-in themes or create your own.
* Customize colors, styles, and even background images.
* Automatically follow your phone’s **day/night mode** or **Material You dynamic colors** (Android 12+).
* Modify **keyboard layouts**, including symbols, numbers, and function keys — or create entirely new ones.

### Multilingual & Feature-Rich

* Type in multiple languages without switching keyboards.
* Optional **glide typing** support (requires a closed-source library you can manually add).
* **Clipboard history** for easy access to recent text.
* **One-handed** and **split keyboard** modes.
* **Number pad** and full backup/restore of settings, learned words, and typing history.

It’s everything you’d expect from a modern keyboard — just without the surveillance.

## Community-Driven and Open Source

HeliBoard thrives on collaboration. You can:

* **Report bugs** or suggest new features on [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/Helium314/HeliBoard/issues).
* **Translate** the app via [Weblate](https://translate.codeberg.org/projects/heliboard/).
* **Share your creations** — from custom color themes to keyboard layouts and dictionaries — with other users in [GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/Helium314/HeliBoard/discussions).

Everything is built in the open, by people who care about user freedom and good software.

## 100% Free, 100% Offline

HeliBoard is open-source and licensed under the **GNU General Public License v3.0 (GPLv3)**.
This means:

* You have the right to view, modify, and share the code.
* Any derivative work must remain equally open and transparent.

Because it’s based on [**AOSP Keyboard**](https://android.googlesource.com/platform/packages/inputmethods/LatinIME/) (Apache 2.0) and [**OpenBoard**](https://github.com/openboard-team/openboard), it inherits the solid foundation of Android’s original input system — refined and extended by the community.
